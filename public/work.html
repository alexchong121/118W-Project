Projects







Planting Trees

Trees and forests are essential to the survival of the planet and it's inhabitants. We are constantly losing breathtakingly amounts of trees each year due to fires and deforestation. Our goal is to help rebuild and sustain our forests by planting trees. Therefore, we have created the Amazon Preservation Association Plant a Tree campaign in which we aim to plant a billion trees.




Preventing Deforestation

Deforestation is leading contributor to the loss of the Amazon rainforest in the whole world depends on. The Amazon rainforest is the world's largest tropical rainforest and contains a a third of the entire planet's plant and animal species. Approximately 50% of the Amazon rainforest could be lost by year 2020 at current rates of deforestation. One of Amazon Preservation Association's main goals is to help prevent and deter deforestation by fighting for corporate accountability and the preservation of the Amazon's ecology.




Fighting Fires

With the increasing global warming, forest fires has become extremely prevalent in today's age. Just in the Amazon alone, there has been a recorded amount of over 40,000 fires. Many of those fires were caused by deforestation by farmers and ranchers. Most of our donations and contributions go straight to local groups and activists to provide aid and combat fires. We also aim to educate to help reduce the risk of fires.




Protecting Endangered Species

The Amazon rainforest houses hundreds of thousands of animals, birds, and insects many of which are endangered species. It contains the highest levels of biodiversity and contains a third of the entire planet's animal and plant species. Their homes and likelihood of survival have constantly been under attack due to fires, deforestation, climate change, and other human activity. Another one of our objectives is to fight against the destruction of their habitats which is causing to endanger those animals.




Helping Indigenous Families

Roughly around 400 different types of indigenous peoples live in and depend on the Amazon rainforest to survive. We aim to help advocate for the rights of the indigenous people that depend on the Amazon rainforest. Amazon Presevation Association campaigns for the indigenous peoples' rights and seek protection for those that vulnerable or threatened.




Preserving Land

Land is lost every single day in the Amazon largely due to deforestation. Our goal is to protect this land by fighing against deforestation, working with local groups and activists, and lobbying for the preservation of the Amazon. We also privately purchase lands that are deemed to be in the greatest danger, so that we can protect those lands and allow local groups to manage and tend to those lands.
