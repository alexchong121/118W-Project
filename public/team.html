Whether it comes to accountancy or law, the board members of Amazon Preservation Association have the proficiency and capability to ensure that they put their best efforts forward when fighting for indigenous rights and all associated with the Amazon.

To safeguard our trustworthiness and autonomy, we reject all donations from national governments, the primary rights violators of tribal individuals/groups. We likewise don't acknowledge donations from any organization that abuses the rights of tribal individuals/groups or is probably going to do as such.

Our headquarters operate out of San Jose State University as can be seen in the map located at the bottom of the page. Currently, we have various missions running in Brazil, Colombia, Bolivia and Peru. As we grow as an organization, we plan to expand to other South America countries that are connected with the Amazon Rainforest.


Our Staff

In order to maintain their safety, the majority of our staff remain anonymous. It also ensures that governments are unable to intervene and prevent them from accessing and assisting indigenous people.

To name a few, members of our staff include regional experts who are connected with tribal communities, animal specialists, those who have agricultural experience and individuals who are tied with other organizations. All members play a crucial role during our missions and are focused on providing the Amazonian communities and tribes with the best possible outcomes
